National Museum of Popular Cultures

The National Museum of Popular Cultures (MNCP) was founded on September 24, 1982 with the purpose of documenting, disseminating and stimulating the initiatives of the creators of popular cultures in Mexico, through dialogue and recognition of pluralism. To commemorate its 40th anniversary, the Ministry of Culture of the Government of Mexico, through the General Directorate of Popular, Indigenous and Urban Cultures (DGCPIU), promotes a series of activities that will motivate reflection on the work of this site.
